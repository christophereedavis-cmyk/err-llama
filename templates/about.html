Errllama
Errl's water free, data center free, local helper.

What is this?

Errllama is a self-hosted AI chat running on a repurposed gaming computer in rural Oregon. No data centers. No cloud. No water-cooled server farms. Just a machine in someone's house, running open source AI models locally, serving anyone who finds their way here.

It's an experiment in what personal computing can look like — useful, private, and unplugged from the infrastructure most AI depends on.

Why does it exist?

Large AI services consume enormous amounts of electricity and water. A single data center can use millions of gallons of water per day for cooling. Errllama uses none. The hardware was already built. The electricity is minimal. The model runs locally and your conversations never leave this machine.

This started as a learning project and turned into something people actually use. That's pretty wild.
